Spaghetti Carbonara

Description

A Roman pasta dish made with simple but high-quality ingredients. It traditionally consists of spaghetti (or other pasta shapes like rigatoni or fettuccine) tossed with a sauce made from egg yolks, Pecorino Romano cheese, guanciale (cured pork jowl), and black pepper. The heat of the pasta cooks the egg yolks to create a creamy sauce without using cream.

Ingredients

  • Spaghetti (or other pasta shapes)
  • Guanciale (cured pork jowl), cut into cubes or strips
  • Egg yolks (ensure they are fresh)
  • Pecorino Romano cheese (finely grated)
  • Black pepper (freshly ground is essential)
  • Salt (use sparingly, as guanciale and Pecorino are salty)

Instructions

  • Prepare the Guanciale
    1. Cut the guanciale into small cubes or strips (about 1/2 to 1 cm).
    2. Add the guanciale to a cold or medium-hot pan (a cast iron or heavy-bottomed pan is good).
    3. Cook over medium-low heat, stirring occasionally, until the guanciale is crispy and has rendered its fat. This can take 10-15 minutes. The key is to render the fat slowly without burning the guanciale.
    4. Remove the crispy guanciale from the pan with a slotted spoon and set aside on a plate. Keep the rendered fat in the pan.
  • Cook the Pasta
    1. Bring a large pot of water to a rolling boil. Add a little salt (remember the guanciale and Pecorino are salty).
    2. Add the spaghetti and cook according to package instructions until al dente (firm to the bite).
  • Prepare the Egg and Cheese Mixture
    1. While the pasta is cooking, whisk together the egg yolks and about half of the grated Pecorino Romano cheese in a bowl until smooth.
    2. Add a generous amount of freshly ground black pepper to the mixture.
  • Combine and Form the Sauce
    1. Just before the pasta is ready, reserve about 1/2 to 1 cup of the starchy pasta water.
    2. Drain the pasta thoroughly.
    3. Immediately add the hot, drained pasta to the pan with the rendered guanciale fat. Toss to coat the pasta evenly.
    4. Remove the pan from the heat. This is crucial to prevent the eggs from scrambling.
    5. Quickly pour the egg and cheese mixture over the hot pasta.
    6. Add a splash of the reserved hot pasta water.
    7. Toss the pasta vigorously with tongs or a fork, continuously mixing, adding more pasta water a tablespoon at a time as needed, until a smooth, creamy sauce forms that coats the pasta. The heat of the pasta will cook the egg yolks without scrambling them.
  • Finish and Serve
    1. Add most of the crispy guanciale back into the pan with the pasta and sauce. Toss to combine.
    2. Serve immediately. Top each portion with the remaining crispy guanciale, more grated Pecorino Romano cheese, and extra black pepper if desired.
